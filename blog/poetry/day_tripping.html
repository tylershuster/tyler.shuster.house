Day Tripping

Day tripping up the coast of California

Past through towns we'd never heard of nor would visit again,

With morning scones on hungover tongues

And folds of pastry dissolving into

the seafood air along eucalyptus avenues and forgotten highways

That the GPS didn't have speed limits for.

Licking smoke on our lips & driving like we had always been machines

Out in the unreasonable December sunshine

Where the waves fell to unseen beaches.

On that unwilling evening we drew salty breaths in the pink-oranging light towards tomorrow

Tyler  Brown
Cifu Shuster
