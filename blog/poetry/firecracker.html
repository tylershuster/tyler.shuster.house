Firecracker

I do not feel your grief and your tears are not mine,

nor is your country.

But I bleed and I bruise, even as you are torn apart.

And I love and I lose, even as you die naked in your streets.

And I hope for more even as you lose all that you once had fighting that your children may live to see a tomorrow.

A tomorrow free of dismembered corpses rotting in ash and a tomorrow where the sun is not obscured by the fire of a nation’s hate against itself—of one man’s crusade.

For we are both men—flesh and blood, though you more than I,

And we both live, though you more than I, even while less.

And we both hope, though you simpler, though you greater.

And we both die, though you sooner, though you never.

My struggles are but firecrackers and I jump to hear them and I bitch and i call my senator and pass a law to outlaw firecrackers.

But yours-yours are the coordinated missile strikes of a man you once trusted

—and all you can do is hope—

hope that a picture of your severed torso

—you and your brother’s(whom you loved so dearly)—

will touch the calloused heart of someone who can change something—anything.

And maybe—maybe—you will have changed the world.

But you will unquestionably have lived a better life than I—a child who jumps at firecrackers.

Tyler  Brown
Cifu Shuster
